Ramadan Mubarak

Ramadan Mubarak to you and your family.

The month of Ramadan is the ninth month in the Hijri calendar and comes after the month of Sha'ban. This month is considered distinct among Muslims from the rest of the months of the Hijri year. It is the month of fasting. In it, people coalesce, communicate a lot, and social relations increase to some extent.

The year 13 before the Hijrah: Laylat al-Qadr, which is the night on which the Qur’an was revealed according to Islamic sources, and the first verse in the Qur’an is (Read), and its different dates are in the odd days of the last ten days of the month, the Hijrah, and on this day also Khadija bint Khuwaylid converted to Islam. Whoever believed in the message of the Prophet of Islam, Muhammad. In the year 1 AH: Hamzah Brigade: The first brigade held by the Messenger of Islam, and that was at the head of seven months of his emigration, on thirty riders, to the coast of the sea. They reached Sayf al-Bahr. So they met and lined up to fight, so Majdi bin Amr al-Juhani walked among them, until the two teams left without a fight.

In the year 2 AH: The Great Battle of Badr: Friday, the 17th of Ramadan, the Qur’an called it the Day of Al-Furqan. Eid prayer began. In the year 2 A.H., Ruqayya bint Muhammad passed away due to an illness that afflicted her during Ramadan. In the year 3 AH: the birth of Imam Al-Hassan bin Ali on the 15th of Ramadan, the third year of the Hijrah. Year 4 AH: Muhammad, the Messenger of Islam, married Zainab bint Khuzaymah. 5 A.H.: The Battle of Banu al-Mustaliq.

In the year 8 AH: the conquest of Makkah. The Great Conquest The Conquest of Makkah: On the twentieth of Ramadan in the year 8 AH. It is called Fath Al-Futuh, as people entered the religion of Islam in droves. In it was the conversion of Abu Sufyan and a large number of the leaders of the polytheists, and in it the order was to demolish the idols around the Kaaba. In the year 9 AH: the arrival of the Messenger of the Kings of Himyar to the Messenger of Islam. Islam is an intellectual delegation. In the year 11 AH: the death of Fatima bint Muhammad. In the year 40 A.H.: The martyrdom of Imam Ali bin Abi Talib, peace be upon him, in Kufa, Abdul Rahman bin Muljim Al-Himyari, in the dawn of the nineteenth of Ramadan, and he died on the twenty-first day of the same month when he was sixty-three years old. In the year 58 AH: the death of the Prophet's wife, Aisha.

Year 92 A.H.: Conquest of Andalusia In the year 702 AH: the Battle of Shaqhab or the Battle of Marj al-Saffar Year 114 A.H.: Battle of the Martyrs’ Court on 1 Ramadan (October 732 A.D.) The battle in the era of the Umayyad Caliph Hisham bin Abdul Malik in the French plains on the banks of the Loire River between Muslims and their leader Abdul Rahman Al Ghafiqi. With this battle, the Muslims lost the last attempt made by the Caliphate to conquer the West and bring Islam to it. It was called The Martyrs Field for the great number of Muslims killed in that battle.>
